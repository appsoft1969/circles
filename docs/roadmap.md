# Product Roadmap

## Product Thesis

Circles should start as a narrow utility for known-group coordination. If it earns repeated organizer usage, it can expand into a broader small-circle operations platform.

## Phase 0: Concept Validation

Goal:

Validate that organizers want a LINE-link-based tool before building a full native app.

Deliverables:

- MVP PRD.
- 5-screen clickable prototype.
- Landing page or waitlist optional.
- Interview script.
- Test with 3 to 5 real organizers.

Validation signal:

- At least 2 organizers are willing to run a real group buy with the prototype or MVP.

## Phase 1: Web MVP

Goal:

Ship a functional mobile-first web app for real group buys.

Scope:

- Organizer signup.
- Circle creation.
- Group buy creation.
- Circle-only member sale creation as a close sibling of group buy.
- Shareable participant link.
- Participant submission without signup.
- Management table.
- Manual payment status.
- Manual pickup status.
- CSV export.
- Duplicate previous group buy.

Out of scope:

- Native iOS/Android apps.
- Payment gateway.
- Logistics.
- Public discovery.
- Chat.
- Social feed.

Validation signal:

- 10 completed group buys.
- At least 30% of organizers create a second group buy.
- Participants can submit without organizer guidance.

## Phase 2: Organizer Workflow Improvements

Goal:

Make repeated usage faster for organizers.

Possible features:

- Saved participant names.
- Reusable item lists.
- Magic-link participant edits.
- Payment reminder messages.
- Better export.
- Group buy templates.
- Template picker for common LINE group tasks.
- Seller/member label for circle-only sales.
- Optional available quantity field.

Validation signal:

- Repeat organizers use templates or duplication.
- Payment tracking becomes a clear reason for continued usage.

## Phase 3: Activity And Signup Templates

Goal:

Expand from group buys into broader small-circle tasks.

Templates:

- Interest check for movie, dinner, sports game, free tickets, or freebies before a plan is confirmed.
- Editable conversion from interest check into event RSVP, poll, or claim registration.
- Claim registration for free tickets, seats, quotas, freebies, or perks.
- Event RSVP.
- Meal gathering.
- Circle-only member sale for self-grown produce, handmade goods, or small-batch items.
- Office meal order.
- Drink order.
- KTV or after-work activity signup.
- Time or location voting.
- Fee split.
- Simple volunteer/task assignment.

Key constraint:

Do not add a social feed or chat. Keep the product task-first.

Validation signal:

- Existing users use at least one non-group-buy template.

## Phase 4: Payments

Goal:

Reduce payment friction only after manual payment tracking is validated.

Options:

- Payment proof upload.
- Transfer last-five-digits field.
- Payment confirmation workflow.
- Local payment provider integration.
- Platform fee or organizer subscription later.

Decision rule:

Do not build payment gateway integration until organizers repeatedly use manual tracking and clearly ask for a smoother collection flow.

## Phase 5: Native App Or PWA Expansion

Goal:

Improve retention and organizer management once the workflow is proven.

Options:

- Installable PWA.
- Native iOS app for organizers.
- Push notifications.
- Home-screen organizer dashboard.

Decision rule:

Build native only when repeat usage justifies the cost. The participant flow should remain accessible from a browser link.

## Long-Term Possibility

If the product works, Circles can become:

> A lightweight operating system for small real-life circles.

Possible future modules:

- Recurring circle activities.
- Shared expenses.
- Member contribution history.
- Simple community treasury.
- Private recommendations.
- Trusted local commerce inside known groups.

These should come later. The first product must stay narrow enough to prove one sharp habit.
